Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Orthotics Fitting

How much do custom orthotics cost at Well Being Chiropractic and does insurance cover them?
Our custom orthotics typically range from $395-595 per pair, depending on your specific needs and the type of orthotic required. Many insurance plans in the Long Island area do provide partial coverage for custom orthotics when deemed medically necessary. We work with most major insurance providers and can verify your coverage before proceeding. We also offer flexible payment plans and FSA/HSA payment options for out-of-pocket expenses.
What's involved in getting fitted for custom orthotics at your Garden City office?
The fitting process takes about 45-60 minutes. First, we perform a comprehensive foot and gait analysis using our state-of-the-art 3D scanning technology. We'll examine how you walk, assess your foot structure, and discuss any pain points or concerns. We then take detailed digital measurements of your feet using our GaitScan system, which captures over 500 points of data per foot. These measurements are used to create your custom orthotics. The entire process, from initial fitting to receiving your orthotics, typically takes 2-3 weeks.
How long do your custom orthotics last and do they come with a warranty?
Our custom orthotics typically last 2-3 years with regular use, depending on your activity level and care of the orthotics. All our orthotics come with a 1-year warranty against defects in materials and workmanship. We also offer a 90-day comfort guarantee - if you're not satisfied with the fit, we'll adjust or remake them at no additional cost. We recommend annual check-ups to ensure your orthotics continue to provide optimal support.
Do I need a referral from my doctor to get custom orthotics at Well Being Chiropractic?
No, you don't need a referral to visit our Garden City office for custom orthotics. As licensed healthcare providers in New York State, our chiropractors can assess and prescribe custom orthotics directly. However, if you're planning to use insurance coverage, some plans may require a referral from your primary care physician. We can help verify your insurance requirements during your initial consultation.
Can I transfer my existing orthotic prescription to Well Being Chiropractic for a replacement pair?
Yes, we can often work with your existing prescription to create replacement orthotics. However, we recommend a brief evaluation appointment at our Garden City office to ensure your current prescription is still meeting your needs, as foot structure and gait patterns can change over time. If you have your previous orthotic specifications, bring them to your appointment. We can also perform a new assessment if needed, which may be covered by your insurance plan.
