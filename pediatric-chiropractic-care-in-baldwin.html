Frequently Asked Questions About Pediatric Chiropractic Care

Do you accept insurance for pediatric chiropractic care?
Yes, Well Being Chiropractic PC accepts most major insurance plans including Empire BCBS, United Healthcare, Cigna, and Aetna. We also work with flexible spending accounts (FSA) and health savings accounts (HSA). Before your child's first visit, we'll verify your coverage and explain any out-of-pocket costs. For families without insurance, we offer affordable self-pay rates starting at $75 per pediatric adjustment.
At what age can my child start receiving chiropractic care?
We safely provide chiropractic care for children of all ages, including newborns. Dr. Johnson has over 15 years of specialized pediatric chiropractic experience and holds additional certifications in pediatric care. Many parents bring their babies in as early as a few weeks old for issues like colic, latching difficulties, or after difficult births. We use extremely gentle techniques specifically designed for infants and children, with pressure no greater than testing the ripeness of a tomato.
How long does a pediatric adjustment appointment typically take?
Initial consultations at our Baldwin office typically take 45-60 minutes. This includes a thorough examination, health history review, and your child's first adjustment if appropriate. Follow-up visits usually last 20-30 minutes. We know children can be anxious about new experiences, so we take time to help them feel comfortable. Our office is kid-friendly with a dedicated play area in the waiting room.
What conditions do you commonly treat in children?
At Well Being Chiropractic PC, we regularly help children with conditions including ear infections, colic, digestive issues, bedwetting, poor posture, sports injuries, and growing pains. We also work with children who have sensory processing disorders, ADHD, and autism spectrum disorders. Each treatment plan is customized to your child's specific needs and development stage. We collaborate with your child's pediatrician when beneficial for their care.
Do I need a referral from my pediatrician to bring my child in?
No, you don't need a referral to bring your child to our Baldwin office. However, some insurance plans may require a referral for coverage. We're happy to communicate with your child's pediatrician and provide progress updates. Many local pediatricians in Nassau County regularly refer patients to us. We can schedule a free 15-minute phone consultation to discuss your child's specific needs and answer any questions before your first visit.
