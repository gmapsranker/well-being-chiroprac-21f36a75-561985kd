Frequently Asked Questions About Prenatal and Postnatal Chiropractic Care

Do I need a referral from my OB-GYN to receive prenatal chiropractic care at Well Being Chiropractic?
No, you don't need a referral to visit our Oceanside office. However, we do recommend informing your OB-GYN or midwife that you're seeking chiropractic care. We frequently collaborate with healthcare providers in Nassau County to ensure coordinated care during your pregnancy. We can also provide you with documentation of your treatment to share with your prenatal care team.
What does the first prenatal adjustment visit cost and what's included?
Your initial visit is $150 and typically takes 60-75 minutes. This includes a comprehensive evaluation, review of your pregnancy history, postural assessment, gentle spinal examination, and your first adjustment if appropriate. We accept most major insurance plans and can verify your coverage before your visit. For self-pay patients, we offer flexible payment plans and packages for ongoing care throughout your pregnancy.
Is chiropractic care safe throughout all stages of pregnancy?
Yes, our chiropractors are specially certified in the Webster Technique and prenatal care. We use pregnancy-specific adjusting tables and techniques that are safe for all trimesters. Our tables have special belly cutouts and adjustable sections to accommodate your changing body. We modify all techniques based on your stage of pregnancy and comfort level. Dr. Williams has over 15 years of experience working with pregnant patients and maintains current certification in prenatal chiropractic care.
How soon after giving birth can I come in for postpartum care?
You can begin postpartum care as soon as you feel comfortable traveling to our office. For vaginal births without complications, many patients come in within 1-2 weeks postpartum. For C-section deliveries, we recommend waiting 3-4 weeks and getting clearance from your doctor. We'll do a gentle evaluation and focus on supporting your recovery, addressing nursing posture, and helping your body adjust to caring for your newborn.
How frequently will I need to come in during my pregnancy?
Treatment frequency varies based on your specific needs and symptoms. Typically, we recommend visits every 2-3 weeks during the first and second trimesters, increasing to weekly visits in the third trimester as your body undergoes more rapid changes. Each visit takes about 20-30 minutes. We're conveniently located near Oceanside Road and offer early morning and evening appointments to accommodate working mothers. We can adjust this schedule based on your symptoms, comfort level, and birth preparation needs.
