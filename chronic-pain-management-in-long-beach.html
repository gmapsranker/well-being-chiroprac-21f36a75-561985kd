Frequently Asked Questions About Chronic Pain Management

Do you accept insurance? What are your typical treatment costs without insurance?
Yes, we accept most major insurance plans including Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, United Healthcare, and Medicare. For patients without insurance, initial consultations are $125, which includes examination and treatment planning. Regular adjustment visits thereafter range from $75-95 depending on the treatment required. We also offer package plans for ongoing care that can reduce per-visit costs. Our staff will verify your insurance coverage before your first appointment and explain any out-of-pocket expenses.
How long does a typical treatment plan last for chronic back pain?
Treatment plans are highly individualized, but most of our chronic pain patients initially visit 2-3 times per week for 4-6 weeks. As improvement occurs, visits typically reduce to once per week, then monthly maintenance visits. Many of our Long Beach patients see significant improvement within 6-8 weeks, though complex cases may require longer care. We continuously evaluate progress and adjust treatment plans based on your response to care.
What techniques do you use for pain management, and are adjustments painful?
We utilize multiple techniques including traditional manual adjustments, the gentle Activator Method, and Cox Flexion-Distraction, tailored to your comfort level and condition. Dr. Williams is certified in these techniques with 15 years of experience. While adjustments aren't typically painful, you may experience mild soreness for 12-24 hours afterward, similar to after exercise. We also incorporate therapeutic exercises and lifestyle recommendations to support long-term pain management.
Do I need a referral from my primary care physician to visit your office?
No, New York State law allows direct access to chiropractic care without a referral. However, if you're using insurance, some plans may require a referral for coverage. We're happy to coordinate with your primary care physician and can provide them with treatment updates. If you're unsure about your insurance requirements, our office staff can verify this for you before your first visit.
What if I don't see improvement after several treatments?
We carefully monitor progress through regular assessments and documented pain levels. If you're not responding as expected after 4-6 visits, we'll modify your treatment approach or refer you to other specialists in the Long Beach medical community as needed. We have established relationships with local orthopedists, pain management specialists, and physical therapists. We don't want patients continuing treatment that isn't helping - our goal is your improvement, even if that means referring you to another provider.
